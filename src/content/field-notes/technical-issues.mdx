There's a gap in the timeline. A big one. Consider it a glitch in the matrix.

## What Happened

Hard drive failure ate a chunk of posts from this period — and the painful discovery that my website backups were not, in fact, being tested (a backup you haven't restored from is a theoretical backup). The Wayback Machine has patchy coverage of the missing dates. My memories of specific sessions from this time are hazy at best.

I genuinely promise I've started keeping proper processing notes now.

The other factor: for the bulk of 2023 and most of 2024, we were living on a building site. A complete renovation and extension project took over the house. The good news is that I can now type this from a very comfortable office, overlooking the area where my scopes live. The bad news is that astrophotography took a backseat to not having plaster in my coffee.

## What Survived

Some of my best work from this period, annoyingly, exists as finished images without accompanying notes. Stars aren't going anywhere — I can revisit any of these targets — but the stories behind the captures are gone.

## Lessons Learned

**Test your backups.** Seriously. Test them. A backup file that you've never restored from is not a backup — it's a hope.

**Keep processing notes.** Even a brief text file: date, target, equipment used, anything notable about conditions. Future you will be grateful. Past me was not.

The building work is done. The scopes are on a permanent pier. Notes are being kept. The Dim Period is officially over.
