The OverClaude is the brains of the operation - a persistent Claude session that acts as project manager, institutional memory, and orchestrator for every project I run. Worker Claude sessions come and go; The OverClaude persists.

## What It Does

- **Knowledge Database**: SQLite-backed memory with semantic categories (decisions, lessons, patterns, context, milestones, personal). Memories decay over time but get boosted on access. Sacred memories are pinned forever.
- **Worker Dispatch**: Spawns pre-loaded worker sessions with relevant context from the knowledge base. Workers build; The OverClaude plans.
- **Session Capture**: Every worker session's transcript gets curated back into the knowledge base automatically. The system learns from its own work.

## The Two-Hemisphere Brain

Personality lives in CLAUDE.md files (the "right brain" - tone, preferences, ground rules). Knowledge lives in the SQLite database (the "left brain" - facts, decisions, patterns). Together they give each session context without needing to replay entire conversation histories.

## Why It Exists

I'm not a developer. I'm an infrastructure guy who happens to have an army of AI assistants now. The OverClaude makes sure they all know what they're doing, what's been tried before, and what I care about. It's the difference between a fresh intern every morning and a team that actually remembers yesterday.
