Living in Newmarket - home of British horse racing - pointing a thousand millimetres of telescope at the Horsehead Nebula felt practically mandatory.

## The Target

The Horsehead (Barnard 33) isn't actually a glowing nebula - it's a dark pillar of dense dust silhouetted against the bright emission nebula IC 434 behind it. The distinctive horse-head profile is a cold molecular cloud, about 1,500 light years away in Orion, that's slowly being eroded by radiation from the nearby bright star Sigma Orionis. It's estimated the head will be gradually worn down and disperse over the next few million years. For now though, it remains one of the most recognisable shapes in the night sky.

Below it sits the Flame Nebula (NGC 2024), powered by the star Alnitak - the easternmost star in Orion's Belt - whose ultraviolet radiation ionises the surrounding gas. The two make a natural pairing in the same field of view at this focal length.
