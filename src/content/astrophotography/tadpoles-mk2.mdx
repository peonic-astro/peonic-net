The Tadpoles Nebula sits in the heart of Auriga, a region packed with emission nebulae that's perfectly placed for winter imaging from the UK. This is my second crack at IC 410 - the first attempt from late 2024 was decent but I knew there was more to pull out of this target with better integration time.

## The Session

Over six nights in March 2025, I stacked up just over 24 hours of narrowband data - split roughly evenly between Ha and OIII. Conditions were typical for my Bortle 6 backyard: sodium glow from Ipswich to the south, the usual light dome from the neighbours, and the occasional cloud that PHD2 would politely pause for.

The EQ6-R was behaving itself this time. Guiding stayed under 0.6" RMS for most of the run, which made a noticeable difference to the star shapes compared to the first attempt.

## Processing

Stacked in APP, processed in PixInsight. The HOO palette gives a natural-looking result for this target - the Ha tadpole structures really pop against the OIII background. Used BlurXTerminator for deconvolution and NoiseXTerminator for noise reduction. Starnet++ for star removal during the non-linear stretch, then recombined.

The trickiest part was balancing the OIII signal in the tadpole tails - they're faint structures and it's easy to over-process them into noise. Less is more here.
