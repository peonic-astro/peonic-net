First real test of the pier setup before summer kicked in. I'd been dealing with some health issues and had taken a chunk of time off work — so this is actually being posted from the future, now that the new website has been built in early autumn.

It's a proper upgrade on my [first attempt](/astrophotography/tadpoles-v1) at this target — more integration time, better equipment, and narrowband mono instead of dual-narrowband on the colour camera. Over 24 hours of Ha and OIII across multiple nights in March.

And yet. Obviously, now I look back at it, I can see loads of issues and things I'm not happy with. The gradients could be cleaner, the star colours could be better, and I know there's more detail hiding in those tadpole tails.

An astrophotographer's work is never done.
