February brought me a Facebook astronomy group, an Object of the Month competition, and a last-minute decision to be an idiot about deadlines.

## The Target

NGC 2359 — Thor's Helmet — is a stellar wind nebula in Canis Major, sitting about 15,000 light years away. It's the result of a massive Wolf-Rayet star (WR 7) blowing a bubble in the surrounding interstellar medium, sculpting it into what looks, with a little imagination, like the winged helmet of a Norse god. The central star is so hot it blasts out stellar wind at over 1,000 km/s — basically a continuous explosion that's been running for thousands of years.

The catch? From my Suffolk backyard, Thor only climbs to about 23 degrees above the horizon. With trees on my southern horizon eating into that further, it's a challenging target.

## The Facebook Nudge

A group member called Andy Thilo posted his work-in-progress image, taken all of 15 miles away from me. It was excellent — clear detail in the bubble shell, crisp star definition. Andy went on to win the competition (well deserved), but his image was the kick I needed.

Problem: I only had four days until the submission deadline.

I decided to go for it anyway. Two nights of data, two integration sessions, 6.67 hours total split across Ha and OIII. Not the 20+ hour deep dive this target deserves, but enough to produce something worth sharing.

## Two Palettes

Most entries in the competition went for the traditional HOO rendition, mapping Ha to red and OIII to blue-green. It's a lovely, natural-looking result for this target. I processed that version too, but with four days and only Ha+OIII data, I wanted to try something different.

The FORAXX palette (sometimes called Foraxx) is a modified narrowband rendering that produces cooler blue-green tones with more contrast in the nebula shell. With just two channels to work with it's a bit of an approximation, but I think the result stands on its own.

## Notes

The low altitude made the later frames noisier as the target approached my treeline — I lost some data there that would have improved the integration. Next time (and there will be a next time) I'll start earlier in the season when Thor sits a bit higher. Getting SII data would also allow a proper SHO rendition and unlock that third channel of colour.
