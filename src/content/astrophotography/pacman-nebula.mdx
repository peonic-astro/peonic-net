The night after [wrestling with tracking gremlins](/astrophotography/unwanted-eggs), I needed to know the fix had actually worked. Enter the Pacman Nebula: a reliable, high-contrast target that shows up tracking problems immediately.

## The Target

NGC 281 - universally known as the Pacman Nebula - is an emission nebula in Cassiopeia about 9,200 light years away. The resemblance to the classic arcade character comes from a dark dust lane that obscures part of the nebula's circular shape, creating the iconic open-mouthed profile. Embedded within it are Bok globules - small, dense dark nebulae that are potential star formation sites - and an open cluster of young hot stars that are illuminating and slowly eroding the surrounding gas.

For narrowband imaging, NGC 281 is a solid target: bright Ha signal, decent OIII, and the SHO palette brings out structure in the dust lanes nicely.

## The Test

41 five-minute subs. Stars round and sharp throughout. No tracking drift, no egg-shaped stars, no sudden loss of guiding lock. The mount was healthy. Crisis resolved.

There's something quietly satisfying about a technical verification image that turns out to be genuinely pretty. This one earns its place on the portfolio.
