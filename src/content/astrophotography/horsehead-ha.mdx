The forecast promised a clear night. The forecast was wrong. This is the consequence.

## What Happened

I had the Horsehead lined up for a full colour narrowband session — Ha, OIII, and SII planned across a long January night. The sky had other ideas. Within a couple of hours, a bank of heavy cloud materialised out of nowhere and that was that.

13 frames of Hydrogen Alpha before the clouds won. 65 minutes of data.

## The Ha View

There's actually something to be said for the Hydrogen Alpha monochrome view of the Horsehead. Without OIII and SII to balance and colour, the Ha signal shows the emission nebula (IC 434) as a glowing bright field, with the dark silhouette of Barnard 33 cutting sharply through it. The Flame Nebula (NGC 2024) in the lower portion of the frame shows good detail. It's a different kind of image to the full colour version — more atmospheric, less information, but visually striking.

The Horsehead itself is a dark absorption nebula — a cold, dense pillar of molecular gas and dust roughly 1,500 light years away in Orion. It only appears as a "nebula" because of the emission region behind it. In Ha, that emission region glows bright, making the silhouette pop.

Definitely coming back to this with a full night's worth of data. The [close-up version in SHO](/astrophotography/horsehead-close) is what that led to eventually — and it was worth the wait.
