What a frustrating couple of months!

## New Kit Frustration

I decided that whilst the ZWO 533MM camera has been an absolute cracker of a device, I wanted something with a higher resolution to pair with the FLT91, whilst keeping the smaller sensor for use with the larger Askar APO.

Having seen some really promising reviews of the PlayerOne range of cameras and not feeling quite ready to drop a significant amount of cash on a no-brand AliExpress camera, I plumped for the Poseidon M Pro setup. Very similar to the famous ASI ZWO 2600MM Pro camera, with a significant reduction in the price tag - the device arrived direct from PlayerOne in under a week.

Here's where the problems started. My FLT91/533MM Pro setup has been bullet-proof reliable pretty much since the day I got it. I had the routine finely tuned and could be ready to image in minutes, especially now it sits on the pier and there's no need for polar alignment.

Switching to the new camera meant a full scope teardown. USB gremlins, EAF and focus mechanism gremlins, issues because muggins here decided to throw off-axis guiding into the mix at the same time, horrible tilt issues with the camera. Given that we'd had a spell of long cloudy weather it also meant that time outside under clear skies trying to get everything sorted took a couple of months - but FINALLY I managed to get everything working again, and despite only having a partially clear night I managed to produce a "first light" image with the new camera.

I have SO many issues with this image - but it's an image from a new camera and after the frustration and swearing that it took to get it, I have to say I'm pretty chuffed at this point.

## Update: January 2026

I often go back and reprocess images - sometimes months or years after original acquisition. Sometimes because I've learned new techniques, sometimes because of new software and plugins, and sometimes simply because I got bored when it was cloudy!

This is one such reprocessing. Using Adam Block's narrowband workflow, I took the standard HOO image and blended in a synthetically coloured SII channel for a much richer result. You can find an excellent YouTube video describing the process [here](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=td4Z4_fIQOE).
