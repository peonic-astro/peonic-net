After the Heart Nebula mosaic went so well, the logical follow-up was the Heart's cosmic neighbour: the Soul Nebula. The two are sometimes called the Heart and Soul of Cassiopeia, and they form a connected complex of star-forming regions about 7,500 light years away.

## The Heart and Soul

IC 1848 (the Soul) and IC 1805 (the Heart) are physically associated — both part of the same larger molecular cloud complex in Cassiopeia. Together they span about 300 light years and contain multiple open star clusters that are actively ionising and sculpting the surrounding gas. The Soul is slightly larger and dimmer than the Heart, but shares the same characteristic structure: bright emission shells, dark pillar structures, and the unmistakeable red-orange glow of hydrogen in narrowband.

The mosaicking itself was the point of this one. Having nailed the four-panel approach on the Heart, I wanted to push the technique further — bigger framing, tighter panel joins, more confidence in the PixInsight mosaic processing.

## Notes

Same setup as the Heart mosaic: WO FLT91, ZWO ASI533MC Pro colour camera, Optolong L-Enhance dual narrowband filter, HEQ5 Pro mount. Four panels of 13 five-minute subs each — 4.33 hours total integration across the mosaic.

The FORAXX palette processing was new territory. I'd been experimenting with it after seeing some excellent results from other imagers, and the Soul is a good target for it — the extended structure responds well to the cooler blue-green tone mapping that FORAXX produces.

This was also the last major image before I started seriously upgrading the kit: the mono camera, the Askar 130PHQ, and the EQ6-R Pro were all waiting in the wings by this point.
