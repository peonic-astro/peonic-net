February was frustrating in the best possible way — not enough clear nights, but the ones I got produced two images I'm genuinely pleased with.

## The Rosette — Going Deeper

The Rosette Nebula (NGC 2237) is one of my recurring targets. It sits in Monoceros about 5,200 light years away, a vast ring of glowing hydrogen gas surrounding a young open star cluster. The cluster's stellar winds have hollowed out the central cavity over the past few million years, leaving the characteristic rose-shaped shell of ionised gas.

This is my second serious attempt at the Rosette, but the first using the longer focal length Askar 130PHQ. The extra reach really changes the target — instead of the whole nebula in frame, this is a close-in look at the heart: the wisps and pillars of dense gas around the central cluster, lit up in Ha, OIII, and SII.

The UK's been oddly warm for February, which sounds great until you realise warm air means atmospheric turbulence and seeing that's more wobbly jelly than hard glass. I had some nights where the stars looked like they were breathing. Managed to pull together 6.67 hours of SHO data across multiple sessions.

## The Headphones — A Tiny Challenge

Jones-Emberson 1 (also, wonderfully, called the Headphones Nebula) is a planetary nebula in Lynx. Planetary nebulae are the final breath of sun-like stars — the outer layers puffed off as the core collapses to a white dwarf, lit from within by ultraviolet radiation. JnEr 1 is old, faint, and diffuse: perfect for the longer focal length but demanding in terms of integration time.

The shape really does look like a pair of headphones if you squint right. Fifty Ha frames and forty-nine OIII frames — 8.25 hours of data — and it's still a subtle target. But the structure is there: two rings connected by a faint bridge, the white dwarf remnant glowing in the middle.

## Notes

Imaging at twice the focal length of my previous setup is noticeably harder — autoguiding needs to be tighter, and everything has to be more precise. I'm getting there, but February's unstable seeing didn't help. I'm starting to get genuinely picky about which data makes the cut, which is progress I suppose.
